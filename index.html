Hi There!

My name is Max Wilhelmi

About me...

I am an artist and programmer who likes bright colors and electronic music. Outdoors are super fun too. I graduated from Michigan State University in fall 2016 with a B.S. in Media and Information & a Minor in Video Game Design.

What can I do?

I like to build user interfaces and make colorful, popping art full of contrast whether it be animated or still. Additionally I can code video game mechanics and make 3D models. Also I'm a crazy good snowboarder.

What do I know?

I have programming knowledge in the languages of C#, Javascript & jQuery, HTML5, and CSS3.
With regard to software, I am proficient in Zbrush, Autodesk Maya, Quixel Suite, the Unity Game Engine, and Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator & After Effects.

Saving Draggy

Saving Draggy

I was strictly an artist on this game, wherein I made a great deal of raster and vector art for the project. I was tasked with character and environmental asset creation and made a series of works which were used for interactive & animated aspects of the game.

Circle of Fifths

Circle of Fifths

For this project I scheduled all of our deadlines and priorities via trello and made sure teammates were on time with their assigned tasks. Additionally, I contributed programming, art, and design assets to the game throughout development including a system which keeps player and enemy combat & UI in sync with the bpm of a given song (written in C#). This game won an award at the 2015 Capital City Film Festival.

Asundr

Asundr

I was one of two artists on this game and did roughly half of the 3d environment models &, their texturing, and their lighting in the game's particular scene. I was responsible for thoroughly playtesting my art and the game as a whole to make sure my additions did not break anything, but also to test for general bugs the team may have missed.

Criminal Procedure

Criminal Procedure

I briefly worked on this project before being transferred to Saving Draggy, on behalf of the GEL Lab determining that Saving Draggy needed more help with art than Criminal Procedure did with programming. During my time with this project, I helped arrange and playtest dialogue throughout the game and made sure the correct audio & subtitles would show up at the appropriate time by using C# in conjunction with game assets.
